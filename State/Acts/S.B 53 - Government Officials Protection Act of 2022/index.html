    S.B 53 Government Officials Protection Act of 2022

    To Redefine Unlicensed Possession Of Non-Civilian Equipment and Gavel Possession

    Be it enacted by the Senate of Mayflower in Congress assembled,

    SECTION I. LONG TITLE; SEVERABILITY; EFFECTIVE DATE¶

    • (a) An act to redefine unlicensed possession of non-civilian equipment to expand its definition and redefine gavel possession.
    • (b) This act shall be severable in that if any section, subsection, clause, sentence, phrase, or word is found to be unconstitutional, the rest of this act shall remain intact unless also found unconstitutional.
    • (c) This act shall take effect upon receiving the Governor’s signature and passage by the Senate

    SECTION II. DEFINITIONS¶

    • (a) The term “Mayflower Criminal Code” shall refer to the Mayflower Criminal Code
    • (b) The term “Gavel Theft Act” shall refer to the Gavel Theft Act. (c) The term “non-civilian equipment” shall refer to telescopic batons, TI26s, handcuffs, radios, radar guns, cones, and barriers.
    • (d) The term “on duty” shall be defined as being on the specific in-game team that has been delegated by the developers as representing a department.

    SECTION III. DOCUMENTATION; ENFORCEMENT¶

    • (a) The Gavel Theft Act shall be repealed
    • (b) The Mayflower Criminal Code shall be amended to have a new charge under “Crimes Against the State” named “Unauthorized possession of a gavel”.
      • (i) It shall be defined as “Any player who possesses a gavel who is not a member of the judiciary or senate”.
      • (ii) It shall be classed as a felony.
      • (iii) It shall carry a sentence of 10 minutes in jail or a fine of $200 dollars.
      • (iv) Gavels shall only be possessed by a person who holds one of the following positions:
        • (1) District Court Judge
        • (2) Supreme Court Justice
        • (3) President of the Senate
        • (4) President Pro Tempore of the Senate
        • (5) Senate Committee Chair
      • (v) It shall be permitted for any individual who holds at least one of the above listed positions to transfer a gavel to another person who also holds at least one of the above listed positions.
      • (vi) Any person who transfers a gavel to another person who does not hold one of the above listed positions shall be guilty of 6 M.C.C. § 1 “Aiding and abetting”.
    • (c) The Mayflower Criminal Code charge 1 M.C.C. § 20 “Unlicensed possession of non-civilian equipment” shall be defined as: “Any player who possesses non-civilian equipment who is not an on duty state certified peace officer, national guardsman, firefighter, park ranger, or transit authority operator”
      • (i) TI26s, handcuffs, and radar guns are only to be possessed by state certified peace officers while on duty of the specific team that the equipment was dispensed upon.
        • (1) Any person who possesses a TI26, or its respective ammunition, handcuff, or radar gun and does not hold one of the above listed positions shall be guilty of 1 M.C.C. § 20 “Unlicensed possession of non-civilian equipment”.
      • (ii) Telescopic batons are only to be possessed by state certified peace officers, national guardsmen, and park rangers while on duty of the specific team that the equipment was dispensed upon.
        • (1) Any person who possesses a telescopic baton and does not hold one of the above listed positions shall be guilty of 1 M.C.C. § 20 “Unlicensed possession of non-civilian equipment”.
      • (iii) Barriers are only to be possessed by state certified peace officers, national guardsmen, park rangers, and firefighters while on duty of the specific team that the equipment was dispensed upon.
        • (1) Any person who possesses a barrier and does not hold one of the above listed positions shall be guilty of 1 M.C.C. § 20 “Unlicensed possession of non-civilian equipment”.
      • (iv) Radios and cones are only to be possessed by state certified peace officers, national guardsmen, park rangers, firefighters, and transit operators while on duty of the specific team that the equipment was dispensed upon.
        • (1) Any person who possesses a radio or cone and does not hold one of the above listed positions shall be guilty of 1 M.C.C. § 20 “Unlicensed possession of non-civilian equipment”.

    SECTION IV. TIMEFRAME¶

    • (a) To be implemented upon the signature of the Governor, or when another method stipulated by the Constitution brings this law into effect.

    SECTION V. NULLIFICATION¶

    • (a) This act may be nullified, repealed, or altered by an Act of Congress.
