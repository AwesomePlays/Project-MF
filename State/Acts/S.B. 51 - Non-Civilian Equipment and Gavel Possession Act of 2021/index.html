    S.B. 51 Non Civilian Equipment and Gavel Possession Act of 2021

    To ensure the well-being of other government officials

    Be it enacted by the Senate of Mayflower in Congress assembled,

    SECTION I. LONG TITLE; SEVERABILITY; EFFECTIVE DATE¶

    • (a) An act to replace the existing Firearm-Free Zone Act.
    • (b) The current Fire-Free Zone Act of 2019 as well as all connecting amendments shall be repealed.

    SECTION II. DEFINITIONS¶

    • (a) The term “firearm” shall be defined as a piece of ordnance designed to fire a projectile at high velocity through the use of explosive force.
    • (b) The term “sidearm” shall be designed as and firearm designed to be carried on a belt or a leg holster.
    • (c) The term “firearm-free zone” shall be defined as the interior of any building or designated area which is designated by a “no firearms permitted past this point” sign or any area specified in this act, where the carrying of a firearm, visibly or concealed, is not permitted and shall extend to the following areas.
      • (i) The Mayflower State House
      • (ii) The Plymouth Courthouse
      • (iii) The Plymouth Townhall
      • (iv) The Westpoint Courthouse
      • (v) Goodison Court Room
      • (vi) The designated property of Camp McEntee
      • (vii) Areas of the New Haven County Sheriff’s Office headquarters that are not designated public access by the following map.
        • (1) This shall not extend to the Goodison Court Room Facilities, which shall be declared a courtroom of the District Court.
      • (viii) The Mayflower National Guard Headquarters located within the City of Lander.
    • (d) The “MCC” shall be defined as the official Mayflower Criminal Code.
    • (e) The term “Carrying a firearm in a firearm-free zone” shall be defined as “any person found carrying a firearm, except when lawfully defending themselves or authorized to do so, within a fire-free zone is guilty of a misdemeanor.
    • (f) The term “Off-Duty” shall be defined as “any member of a government or county organization that is on the ‘civilian’ team.”

    SECTION III. DOCUMENTATION; ENFORCEMENT¶

    • a) Carrying a firearm in a firearm-free zone shall remain within the MCC under the “Crimes Involving Firearms” section.
    • (b) Carrying a firearm in a firearm-free zone shall be recognized as a misdemeanor, punishable by a citation of $225 or a sentence of 17 minutes in the county jail.
    • (c) This shall not extend to members of Law Enforcement Agencies, members of an Emergency Service, or members of the National Guard executing their lawful duties.
      • (i) Off-duty Law Enforcement Officers, members of emergency services, and members of the National Guard shall be permitted to carry a sidearm, including a department-issued Bennetti 17 provided department policy allows it, into firearm free zones with the exception of
        • (1) The State House
        • (2) The property of Camp McEntee without explicit permission from the sitting Governor of Mayflower.
      • (ii) This shall not extend to members of the public, when the law permits, using their firearm for the purpose of self-defense or to combat an active threat.
      • (iii) This shall not extend to members of the Judiciary, Clerks of the District Court, members of the Department of Justice, or any bar-certified attorney when entering any District Courthouse or District Courtroom within the state.
      • (iv) This shall not extend to members of the Mayflower Legislature, including Clerks of the Senate when entering the Senate Chambers and the State House.
      • (v) This shall not extend to Justices of the Supreme Court and Clerks of the Supreme Court when entering the State House.
    • (d) Any person found in violation of Section III(c) may be charged with Carrying a firearm in a firearm free zone.
    • (e) Law Enforcement may have the discretion of determining whether or not a person is unlawfully carrying a firearm in a firearm-free zone as defined in Section III(c).
    • (f) A firearm-free zone in a public area may only be established by the mayflower State Government via legislation passed through Congress.

    SECTION IV. TIMEFRAME¶

    • (a) To be implemented upon the signature of the Governor, or when another method stipulated by the Constitution brings this law into effect.

    SECTION V. NULLIFICATION¶

    • (a) This act may be nullified, repealed, or altered by an Act of Congress.
