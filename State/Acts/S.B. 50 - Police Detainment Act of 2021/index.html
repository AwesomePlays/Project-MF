    S.B. 50 Police Detainment Act of 2021

    Police Detainment Act 2021

    Be it enacted by the Senate of Mayflower in Congress assembled,

    SECTION I. LONG TITLE; SEVERABILITY; EFFECTIVE DATE¶

    • (a) An Act to formerly update and nullify the current Police Detainment Act of 2020.
    • (b) This act shall be severable so that if any word, sentence, part, or section is found to be unconstitutional, the rest of the act shall be preserved unless also declared to be unconstitutional.
    • (c) This act shall take effect upon receiving the Governor’s signature and passing the Senate.

    SECTION II. DEFINITIONS¶

    • (a) The term “M.C.C.” shall refer to the Mayflower Criminal Code.
    • (b) The term “detain” shall refer to a situation where a law enforcement officer, who has reasonable suspicion that a crime has been committed, legally orders a person who is being investigated or held to not leave their immediate presence.
    • (c) The term “arrest” shall refer to a situation where a law enforcement officer, who has probable cause that a crime has been committed, places or holds a suspect in custody pending criminal charges but has not booked them yet.
    • (d) The term “book” shall refer to when a criminal is formally sent to jail using the in-game terminal and GUI.
    • (e) The term “Traffic Stop” shall refer to a situation where a law enforcement officer stops a motor vehicle and detains the driver and/or passengers.
    • (f) The term “Non-Traffic Stop” shall refer to a situation where a law enforcement officer detains a person on foot.
    • (g) The term “Interrogation” shall refer to a situation where a law enforcement officer detains or arrests a person, informs them of their Miranda Rights, and conducts a series of questions directed towards the person.
    • (h) The term “supervisor” shall refer to a person holding a supervisory rank in any department.
    • (i) The term “Supervisor Request” shall refer to a situation where a detained or arrested person requests a supervisor employed by the same department that the detaining or arresting law enforcement officer is employed by.
    • (j) The term “Transport” shall refer to a situation where a suspect has been arrested and is in the process of being transferred to an appropriate booking terminal.

    SECTION III. DOCUMENTATION; ENFORCEMENT¶

    • (a) Traffic Stop
      • (i) When a law enforcement officer pulls over a motor vehicle and conducts a traffic stop. The stop may last at most 12 minutes without charging them.
        • (1) If the time goes over 12 minutes, all charges on the suspect shall be voided.
    • (b) Non-Traffic Stop
      • (i) When a law enforcement officer stops a person in any setting, they shall have the right to conduct a non-traffic stop on the person for no more than 12 minutes without charging them with a crime.
        • (1) If the time goes over 12 minutes, all charges on the suspect shall be voided.
    • (c) Interrogations
      • (i) When a law enforcement officer conducts an interrogation on a person, the person shall not be questioned for more than 45 minutes. Once the interrogation has been concluded, the law enforcement officer has an additional 15 minutes to charge the person with a crime.
        • (1) If the time limit goes over the additional charging period of 15 minutes then all the charges shall be voided.
      • (ii) If the person requests a bar certified attorney, the time limit shall be halted. Upon the arrival of the person’s requested bar certified attorney or a public defender tasked with representing the person, the time limit shall resume.
      • (iii) If the time limit goes over the 45 minute limit, the person may not be asked any more questions and the interrogation shall be concluded.
        • (1) Does not apply if the person willingly continues the interrogation. After 45 minutes, a person shall be free to leave if not being charged with a crime
      • (iv) This time limit shall be extended by 45 minutes if the interrogation is being conducted upon a person employed by the department the interrogator is employed by. At the end of the 90 minutes (original 45 + 45 outlined here), the law enforcement officer has an additional 15 minutes to charge the suspect with a crime.
        • (1) If the time limit goes over the additional charging period of 15 minutes then all the charges shall be void.
    • (d) Supervisor Requests
      • (i) If a detained person requests a supervisor, any time limit shall be halted until the supervisor has concluded the supervisor request.
      • (ii) Upon a supervisor dismissing a supervisor request, the original time limit that was in place shall continue where it had left off.
      • (iii) A supervisor shall have full discretion on when to terminate a supervisor request and continue with booking or other actions covered in this act such as interrogation or release.
      • (iv) Departments may establish regulations and policies in accordance with this
    • (e) Warrants
      • (i) Any time a person is subject to any type of warrant, no time limit shall be placed upon their arrest, interrogation, or any other action specified by a time limit in this act.
      • (ii) Any time a law enforcement officer submits paperwork to a judge to receive a warrant for a subject, any time limit placed upon their arrest, interrogation, or any other action specified by a time limit in this act shall be halted.
    • (f) Uncooperative Situations

    SECTION IV. TIMEFRAME¶

    • (a) To be implemented upon the signature of the Governor, or when another method stipulated by the Constitution brings this law into effect.

    SECTION V. NULLIFICATION¶

    • (a) This act may be nullified, repealed, or altered by an Act of Congress.
