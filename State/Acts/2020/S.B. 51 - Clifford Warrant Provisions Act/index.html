    S.B. 51 Clifford Warrant Provisions Act

    An act to modernize the Warrant System.

    Introduced by Mr. OmniGalaxy and Mr. Dino_Raf

    Be it enacted by the Senate of Mayflower in Congress assembled,

    SECTION I. LONG TITLE; SEVERABILITY; EFFECTIVE DATE¶

    • (a) An act to better facilitate the issuance of warrants by state judges
    • (b) This act shall remain severable, and if any section, sub-section, clause, phrase or word is found to be unconstitutional the remainder of this act shall remain intact unless also found to be unconstitutional

    Constitutional Status

    The Mayflower Supreme Court has nullified certain provisions within II(b), II(e) as they have been deemed unconstitutional (06-02).

    SECTION II. DEFINITIONS¶

    • (a) A warrant shall be defined as either a search or arrest warrant defined herein.
    • (b) A Search warrant shall be defined as a legal order by a District Court Judge authorizing the search of property. Illegally obtained items may be seized by Law Enforcement Officers during said warrant.
    • (c) A law enforcement officer shall be defined as any state certified peace officer serving within a law enforcement agency.
    • (d) A Department of Justice Official shall be defined as anyone within the Department of Justice group linked herein that possesses a rank above Legal Staff.
    • (e) An Arrest warrant shall be defined as a legal order by a District Court Judge authorizing the detainment and arrest of an individual for a crime pursuant to the Mayflower Criminal Code, the Mayflower Vehicle Code or the County Code.
    • (f) The Sheriff’s Office shall be defined at a group located herein or otherwise prescribed by law.
      • (i) The County Sheriff shall be defined as the Sheriff’s Office elected leader, pursuant to the Mayflower Constitution and County Charter.
    • (g) An officer of the court shall be defined as any state-employed and/or state-certified individual charged with the administration of justice in a courtroom setting;
      • (i) This shall extend to sitting judges, law enforcement officers charged with courtroom protection, representatives of the State, or judicial employees in the course of their duties.

    SECTION III. DOCUMENTATION; ENFORCEMENT¶

    • (a) District Court Judges shall be permitted to author warrants only upon pragmatic request by a law enforcement officer or Department of Justice Official;
      • (i) The Chief Justice of the Supreme Court shall determine the means by which affidavits are submitted;
      • (ii) Any person subject to a warrant may directly appeal to the District Court of the State of Mayflower;
        • (1) The Supreme Court shall determine the rules and procedures for the conditions of appeal.
        • (2) Search warrants may be only issued upon reception of a sworn affidavit;
          • a) The sworn affidavit must provide incontestable reasoning that demonstrates an alleged individual is in possession of an item deemed unlawful pursuant to the Mayflower Criminal Code;
          • b) The reasoning must include unaltered evidence;
            • i) Such evidence must include elements that denote its timeliness;
            • ii) No evidence may exceed ten (10) days’ time;
            • iii) Such evidence must include the subject’s username, as well as elements that demonstrate the individual’s possession of an item is in contravention of state law;
          • c) The affidavit must be submitted by a state law enforcement officer, an officer of the Court, or a Bar certified employee of the Department of Justice who is involved with relevant proceedings;
          • d) The County Sheriff shall facilitate search warrants;
          • e) The warrant shall include reason for each of its search provisions;
            • i) The judge shall determine, in good reason, the property of an alleged subject to be searched;
          • f) No judge shall order the seizure of items that were considered lawful at the time of the evidence’s collection.
        • (3) Arrest warrants may be only issued upon reception of a sworn affidavit;
          • a) The sworn affidavit must provide incontestable reasoning that demonstrates an alleged individual is in possession of an item deemed unlawful pursuant to the Mayflower Criminal Code;
            • i) The reasoning must include unaltered evidence;
              • 1) Such evidence must include elements that denote its timeliness;
              • 2) No evidence may exceed two (2) days’ time;
                • a. Such evidence must include the subject’s username, as well as elements that demonstrate the subject has contravened state law in such a manner that degrades the criminal justice system.
            • ii) Such reasoning must demonstrate an urgency in the interest of the law, as defined by the undersigned judge.
          • b) The affidavit may not originate from a sitting judge who is the primary witness of a crime;
          • c) No arrest warrant shall be signed against a subject who has already been arrested or charged with the presented evidence.
        • (4) The judge shall recuse itself from the signature and procedures therein of a warrant should he maintain an occupational relationship with the affidavit’s undersigned.
      • (iii) Warrants shall be executed in an expedient manner as determined by the judge;
        • (1) No judge shall be present for the execution of warrants in which they have undersigned.
        • (2) The County Sheriff may dedicate a bureau or division to the execution of warrants;
          • a) Such bureau shall have total jurisdiction over the execution of search warrants;
          • b) The undersigned judge may, in the case of an arrest warrant, confer the responsibility of a warrant’s execution unto any state-certified law enforcement officer.
        • (3) The procedure of its execution may be determined by the undersigned judge

    SECTION IV. TIMEFRAME¶

    • (a) To be implemented upon the signature of the Governor, or when another method stipulated by the Constitution brings this law into effect.

    SECTION X. NULLIFICATION¶

    • (a) This Act can be nullified by either a superseding act passed by Congress, or if repealed by an act of Congress.
    • (b) Any provision of this act found to be unconstitutional by the relevant authority will be null and void.
    • (c) Specific clauses in existing legislation at all levels of government that contradict this legislation shall be considered null and void
