    S.B. 53 The Seventh Public Servants Act

    The Seventh Public Servants Act

    Introduced by Mr. OmniGalaxy and Mr. LordHenryVonHenry

    Be it enacted by the Senate of Mayflower in Congress assembled,

    SECTION I. LONG TITLE; SEVERABILITY; EFFECTIVE DATE¶

    • (a) An act to replace the existing Public Servants Act with an updated version, expanding the number of jobs able to be held, while eliminating existing exemptions.
    • (b) The Sixth Public Servants Act, and connected amendments shall be repealed.
    • (c) The Misconduct Act section III (b) shall be amended to read:
      • (i) “Gross Misconduct” shall be considered a Felony, and shall result in 15 minutes within the County Jail or a $500 fine.

    SECTION II. DEFINITIONS¶

    • (a) The term “job” in the context of employment restrictions in this act, shall be considered employment under any department or law enforcement agency, or any Government Position.
    • (b) The term “termination” shall refer to the cessation of one’s employment, as per the decision of an employing authority.
    • (c) The following shall not count towards employment restrictions in this act:
      • (i) Members of the Mayflower State Bar
      • (ii) Clerks of the Supreme Court
      • (iii) Clerks of the District Court
      • (iv) Clerks of the County Board
      • (v) Clerks of a Municipal Council
      • (vi) Clerks of the Senate
      • (vii) The Sergeant At Arms of the Senate
      • (viii) Members of ICF
      • (ix) Part-Time Employees of the Mayflower Public Broadcasting Service
    • (d) The term “Law Enforcement Agency” shall refer to any of the following:
      • (i) Mayflower State Police
      • (ii) New Haven County Sheriff’s Office
      • (iii) Lander Police Department
      • (iv) Plymouth Police Department
    • (e) The term “Department” shall refer to any of the following:
      • (i) Mayflower Department of Justice
        • (1) Both the Attorney General’s Office and the District Attorney’s Office.
      • (ii) Mayflower Law Enforcement Training Institute
        • (1) Only counts towards employment restrictions if Chief Instructor or higher, Employee Rights still apply.
      • (iii) Mayflower Public Broadcasting Service
      • (iv) New Haven County Transit Authority
      • (v) New Haven County Fire Department
      • (vi) Mayflower Department of Parks and Wildlife
      • (vii) Department of State
      • (viii) Mayflower National Guard
    • (f) A “Department Head” shall be considered to be the head of any Law Enforcement Agency or the head of any organisation listed as a department.
    • (g) A “Government Position” shall refer to any of the following:
      • (i) Governor
      • (ii) Lieutenant Governor
      • (iii) Cabinet Member
      • (iv) Attorney General
      • (v) Chief Justice
      • (vi) Associate Justice
      • (vii) Chief Judge
      • (viii) District Court Judge
      • (ix) State Senate
      • (x) County Board
      • (xi) County Executive
      • (xii) Municipal Mayor
      • (xiii) Municipal Council
      • (xiv) Any employment under State, County, or Municipal Government that has any form of defined power or responsibilities by law, charter, or ordinance that is otherwise not mentioned by this act.
    • (h) A “Member of the Judiciary” shall be considered to be any of the following:
      • (i) A District Court Judge
      • (ii) A Supreme Court Justice

    SECTION III. EMPLOYMENT RESTRICTIONS¶

    • (a) The following are employment restrictions, which no one, employee or employer, should breach:
      • (i) (i) A person can only be a member of one law enforcement agency.
      • (ii) A person cannot hold more than three jobs.
        • (1) Any one job can only count as one, this only applies if the connected job is given automatically by law, for example if the Attorney General has a position in the cabinet due to being Attorney General automatically by law, it would only count as one job.
      • (iii) A person cannot hold a Government position and be a Department Head.
        • (1) This does not apply if the position in Government is given automatically by law due to the department head role for example Sheriff being a member of the County board or Municipal Police Chiefs being members of their relevant Municipal Council.
      • (iv) A person cannot be more than one Department Head.
      • (v) A person cannot hold more than one job defined as a Government Position.
        • (1) This includes those who get a Government Position automatically by law due to another position.
    • (b) Ethics Restrictions Any “Member of the Judiciary” shall recuse themselves from any case involving a department, law enforcement agency or organisation they are a member of where this would still allow the case to be heard by an alternative member.
    • (c) Part-Time Employees of the Mayflower Public Broadcasting Service are not able to participate in, nor contribute to, any Mayflower Public Broadcasting Service functions/services outside of utilizing basic equipment provided to Part-Time Employees in New Haven County

    SECTION IV. EMPLOYEE RIGHTS¶

    • (a) No citizen shall be denied employment except for the following reasons:
      • (i) Lacking the relevant qualifications.
      • (ii) Crimes, Misdemeanor or Felony, currently on their record.
    • (iii) History of breaching department policy within the past 12 months.
    • (b) No employee shall be discriminated against, treated differently, demoted, suspended, or otherwise punished, except for the following:
      • (i) Breaching department policy or “good practice” within the previous three weeks.
      • (ii) Breaching department policy or “good practice” within the previous six weeks, but the event had only come to light within the previous three weeks.
      • (iii) Crimes, Misdemeanor or Felony, committed since the employment began.
      • (iv) Crimes, Misdemeanor or Felony, they have been convicted of by the courts, since the employment began.
      • (v) If the department, organisation, company (public or private) or law enforcement agency is being restructured.
        • (1) Unless it can be proved any specific demotion or termination was issued unfairly and/or was specifically targeted against one or more employees.
    • (c) No employee shall be terminated except for the following reasons:
      • (i) Illegality of employment.
      • (ii) Breaching department policy or “good practice” within the previous month.
      • (iii) Breaching department policy or “good practice” within the previous two months, but the event had only come to light within the previous three weeks.
      • (iv) Inactivity or Incompetence.
      • (v) Crimes, Misdemeanor or Felony, committed since the employment began.
      • (vi) Crimes, Misdemeanor or Felony, they have been convicted of by the courts, since the employment began.
      • (vii) If the department, organisation, company (public or private) or law enforcement agency is being restructured.
        • (1) Unless it can be proved any specific demotion or termination was issued unfairly and/or was specifically targeted against one or more employees.
    • (d) An Employee shall be entitled to take a leave of absence up to the length of one week, without consequence. Employees may request no more than one leave of absence every four weeks.
      • (i) An employee may, at the discretion of their employer, be granted an extended leave of absence, without consequence.

    SECTION V. DOCUMENTATION; ENFORCEMENT¶

    • (a) Any employee who violations Section III Employment Restrictions, shall be guilty of “Obstruction of Justice”.
    • (b) Any employer who by employing an employee, violates Section III Employment Restrictions, shall be guilty of “Obstruction of Justice”.
    • (c) Any employer who intentionally breaches any part of section IV shall be guilty of Gross Misconduct.
    • (d) In cases where the head of the organisation, company (public or private), or department did not directly violate Section IV Employee Rights, and a subordinate did, both the subordinate and the head shall be considered the employer and hence, providing they were involved in breaching the Employee’s rights, liable.
    • (e) Any decision made by an employer which violates Section IV Employee Rights, shall be susceptible to nullification or reversal by the Office of the Attorney General.
    • (f) Any Government Official who breaches Section III Employment Restrictions, shall immediately cease all operations and duties related to their Government Position until they have either been removed, or are no longer in violation of the section. Failure to do this shall be considered “Gross Misconduct”.
    • (g) Any Law Enforcement Officer who breaches Section III Employment Restrictions, shall immediately cease all operations and duties related to their Law Enforcement Position until they have either been removed, or are no longer in violation of the section. Failure to do this shall be considered “Gross Misconduct”.
    • (h) Any Employee who is in violation of Section III Employment Restrictions, who does not immediately rectify the situation through one or more resignations, shall be guilty of “Gross Misconduct”.

    SECTION VI. TIMEFRAME¶

    • (a) To be implemented upon the signature of the Governor, or when another method stipulated by the Constitution brings this law into effect.

    SECTION VII. NULLIFICATION¶

    • (a) This act may be nullified or repealed by an Act of Congress.
