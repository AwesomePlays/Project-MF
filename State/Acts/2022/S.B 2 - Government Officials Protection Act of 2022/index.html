    S.B 2 Government Officials Protection Act of 2022

    Introduced by Mr. Guarost - 10th Congress, 2nd Week

    An act to ensure the well-being among government officials

    Be it enacted by the Senate of Mayflower in Congress assembled,

    SECTION I. LONG TITLE; SEVERABILITY; EFFECTIVE DATE¶

    • (a) An act to ensure security and safety among the government officials.
    • (b) This act shall remain servable, and if any section, sub-section, clause, phrase or word is found to be unconstitutional the remainder of this act shall remain intact unless also found to be unconstitutional.
    • (c) The Mayflower State Police Act shall not be amended, repealed, nor considered modified by the content of this act.
    • (d) This act shall go into effect in accordance with Section IV(a) of this act.

    SECTION II. DEFINITIONS¶

    • (a) “Jurisdiction” shall be defined as the area where a law enforcement agency or department is limited to patrol or execute duties.
    • (c) “Government officials” shall be defined in this act as those who:
      • (i) Are members of the Governor's Cabinet;
      • (ii) Are Senators;
      • (iii) Are Judges;
      • (iv) The Lieutenant Governor; and
      • (v) The Governor.
    • (d) “Local law enforcement" shall be defined as those law enforcement agencies who have jurisdiction over a specific area within their respective county.
      • (i) The Mayflower State Police shall not fall under this definition.
    • (e) “Credible threat” shall be defined as a threat targeted toward a single person or persons which has been decided likely to deal harm to those involved.
    • (f) "Protective services” shall be defined as the lawful actions taken by a local authority to guarantee the security and safety of a government official.

    SECTION III. DOCUMENTATION; ENFORCEMENT¶

    • (a) Local law enforcement shall be permitted to offer protective services to a government official upon request.
      • (i) A Government official may dismiss their protection at any time, at their sole discretion.
    • (b) Local law enforcement may only deny protective services if:
      • (i) Providing protection to the official would compromise the security and safety of the State, local Government and/or the wider public.
      • (ii) All local law-enforcement are preoccupied with:
        • (1) Transporting an arrested/detained person;
        • (2) Responding to an active call; or
        • (3) Any other official duty.
      • (iii) The official is already receiving protection from another Law Enforcement Agency; or
      • (iv) There is no credible threat to the government official.

    SECTION IV. TIMEFRAME¶

    • (a) To be implemented upon the signature of the Governor, or when another method stipulated by the Constitution brings this law into effect.

    SECTION V. NULLIFICATION¶

    • (a) This act may be nullified, repealed, or altered by an Act of Congress.
